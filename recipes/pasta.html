Pasta Recipe

Description

pasta, any of several starchy food preparations (pasta alimentaria) frequently associated with Italian cuisine and made from semolina, the granular product obtained from the endosperm of a type of wheat called durum, and containing a large proportion of gluten (elastic protein).

It is formed into ribbons, cords, tubes, and various special shapes, all originally developed for specific characteristics, such as ability to retain heat or hold sauces.

Ingredients

  • 2 boneless skinless chicken breast halves, cut into thin strips
  • 4 ounces linguine, cooked al dente
  • 2 teaspoons cajun seasoning (your recipe, Cajun Seasoning Mix or store-bought)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 thinly sliced green onion
  • 1⁄2 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 1 ⁄ 4 cup tomato sauce
  • 2 tablespoons chopped sun-dried tomatoes
  • 1⁄4 teaspoon salt
  1. Place chicken and Cajun seasoning in a bowl and toss to coat.
  2. In a large skillet over medium heat, sauté chicken in butter or margarine until chicken is tender, about 5 to 7 minutes.
  3. Reduce heat add green onion, heavy cream, tomatoes, basil, salt, garlic powder, black pepper and heat through.
  4. Pour over hot linguine and toss with Parmesan cheese.
